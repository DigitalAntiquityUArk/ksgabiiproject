Is there a pattern to grave orientation at Gabii?

Scientific disciplines are all about asking question. Our questions were "Is there some kind of correlation between the way graves are oriented directionally and what ceramics and grave goods they contain?" and "Are there distribution patterns associated with different kinds of ceramics specifically?"

But when you ask a question, you don't always get the answer you expect. Did grave orientation follow a pattern at Gabii? Not at all. However, it did adhere to a larger cultural pattern.

J.M.C. Toynbee says of Roman cemeteries—

The alignments of the various groups of tombs in a single cemetery are, however, sometimes so different from one another that we can only deduce the absence of any public control of a cemetery's growth and of any overall rational planning of it as an entity

North-South

There are ten graves with a North-South orientation in Area B. 50% of these contain grave goods.

East-West

There are twice as many East-West graves as North-South, but only 15% have grave goods.

Ceramics

Ceramics were included in only 36.6% of graves at Area B in total.

Grave Goods

26.6% of graves contained grave goods besides ceramics—this includes figurines, rings, and mosaic tile.
